The [tears on the cheeks](https://www.politico.com/news/2022/04/26/twitters-top-lawyer-reassures-staff-cries-during-meeting-about-musk-takeover-00027931) of some staff have barely had time to dry but that hasn't stopped Elon Musk from voicing some of the changes he'd like to see at his new social media company, Twitter, including:

* [for it to be](https://twitter.com/elonmusk/status/1519415674111672325) "politically neutral, which effectively means upsetting the far right and the far left equally";
* for [private messages to](https://twitter.com/elonmusk/status/1519469891455234048) "have end to end encryption like Signal, so no one can spy on or hack your messages";
* "[making the algorithms](https://www.prnewswire.com/news-releases/elon-musk-to-acquire-twitter-301532245.html) open source to increase trust";
* to "[defeat](https://twitter.com/elonmusk/status/1517215066550116354) the spam bots or die trying"; and
* to "[authenticate](https://twitter.com/elonmusk/status/1517215736606957573) all real humans".

Those goals are all easier tweeted than done. For instance, "political neutrality" might upset [European regulators](https://techcrunch.com/2022/04/26/elon-musk-free-speech-regulation/) and encrypting private messages could negatively affect revenue, which is [almost entirely](https://www.statista.com/statistics/274566/twitters-annual-revenue-by-channel/) advertising-based.

On the latter point, in a couple of since-deleted tweets Musk did [suggest](https://www.cnbc.com/2022/04/10/elon-musk-on-changes-to-twitter-blue-says-dogecoin-should-be-accepted.html) that he had plans to shake-up not just how Twitter works but also how it makes money, pivoting it away from advertising towards subscriptions. According to Musk:

> "Everyone who signs up for Twitter Blue (ie pays $3/month) should get an authentication checkmark", and there should be "no ads" because "The power of corporations to dictate policy is greatly enhanced if Twitter depends on advertising money to survive."

That would be a major shakeup for Twitter and would set it apart from the social media 'model', which has involved the monetisation of 'free' products almost exclusively through data collection and advertising.

Interestingly, the only tweets that remain from the above exchange are of Musk replying to people [pondering what to charge](https://twitter.com/elonmusk/status/1512964005060657156) and whether to accept crypto, with Musk [responding](https://twitter.com/elonmusk/status/1512954808394731521): "Maybe even an option to pay in Doge\[coin]?"

Time will tell whether Musk will be able to radically transform Twitter, because that's probably what's going to be required to turn around a company that has [struggled](https://twitter.com/benedictevans/status/1514555699208065034) to ship anything or attract [new users](https://backlinko.com/twitter-users) for the past several years. 

Of course that all assumes the deal closes, which is no guarantee when an eccentric character like Musk is involved!
