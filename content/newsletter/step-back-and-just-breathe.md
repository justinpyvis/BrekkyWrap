> "Step back and just breathe."

Those were the words uttered by Reserve Bank of New Zealand (RBNZ) governor Adrian Orr [yesterday](https://www.stuff.co.nz/business/128548000/adrian-orr-to-mps-on-inflation-step-back-and-just-breathe), "who was in unapologetic and robust form" as he testified to the NZ Parliament's Finance and Expenditure select committee:

> "Given the nature and scale of the economic shocks we have come through, I am positively surprised how well we have come through this activity.
>
> We cannot do anything about global oil prices.
>
> We have got this. It is business as usual."

The RBNZ has an annual inflation target of 1 to 3% but it's currently [running at 6.9%](https://brekkywrap.com/2022/even-further-behind/), with trimmed mean measures – "which exclude extreme price movements" – also rising from between 5.2 to 6.5%.

 But don't worry – despite being consistently wrong for over a year (remember [when inflation was](https://www.rbnz.govt.nz/monetary-policy/monetary-policy-statement/mps-august-2021) "largely temporary"?), Orr still wants people to trust that "*we have got this"*!

Orr also appeared to be completely dismissive of the role fiscal stimulus, not adequately offset by tighter monetary policy (real rates are still negative), played in pumping up prices. That's despite the RBNZ's own [forecasts](https://www.rbnz.govt.nz/monetary-policy/monetary-policy-statement) predicting a positive "[output gap](https://www.imf.org/external/pubs/ft/fandd/2013/09/basics.htm)" – the difference between the actual output of an economy and its potential output – until 2025.

In fact, Orr wants it to remain that way:

> "When we're looking at monetary policy and saying 'where's the biggest impulses to change in pace coming from', at present, it's not the Government.
>
> I would say strongly we are in for significant economic and financial challenges around which traditional fiscal support is necessary to enable society to function as normal."

Quite extraordinary. Do watch the [full testimony](https://www.facebook.com/FESCNZ/videos/706688640612966/) if you have the time (and patience).
