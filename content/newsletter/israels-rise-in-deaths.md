Israel is currently [going through](https://www.worldometers.info/coronavirus/country/israel/) its fifth and most deadly wave of the pandemic, leading some ‚Äì such as our very own [Clive Palmer](https://www.facebook.com/CliveFrederickPalmer/videos/listen-to-my-latest-press-conference-talking-about-all-things-politics-and-polic/412880380552657/) ‚Äì to claim that:

> "And the other lies that are being told is that if you go to Israel today, 94% of all people in hospital have had the double jab. So it it doesn't cut down on hospitalisation, it doesn't stop you getting it, it doesn't stop you passing it on."

In Israel there [are currently](https://datadashboard.health.gov.il/COVID-19/general?tileName=SeriousVaccinationStatusDaily) 513 unvaccinated, 131 partially vaccinated and 557 vaccinated seriously ill COVID-19 patients in hospital. The divide is even greater if we filter for only those aged over 60 (which make up 82% of hospitalisations), where the seriously ill number 390 unvaccinated, 109 partially vaccinated and 485 vaccinated.

In other words, 49.3% of the over-60s who are severely ill are vaccinated while just 39.6% are unvaccinated. It's not as drastic as Palmer claimed but certainly *appears* to support his point, until you look at [one more chart](https://datadashboard.health.gov.il/COVID-19/general?tileName=vaccinatedByAge) which shows that the over 60 population is almost 90% vaccinated.

When we adjust for the [denominator effect](https://brekkywrap.com/2021/denominators/) (seriously ill patients per 100,000 people), the number becomes 403 unvaccinated, 128 partially vaccinated and just 35 vaccinated.

There are a few lessons we can take from these data, other the obvious insight that you may want to think twice before taking health advice from Clive Palmer. 

Perhaps the most positive takeaway is that virtually no one under the age of 60 is seriously ill, which should give jurisdictions where the elderly are heavily vaccinated, such as Australia, the confidence to stay open: the existing vaccines are excellent at preventing severe disease.

But another other important insight is that existing vaccines ‚Äì even booster shots ‚Äì don't seem to do much to prevent the transmission of Omicron. While some of that might be because Israel has a relatively low vaccination rate amongst the prime spreaders (those aged 20-49 are only ~76.5% vaccinated), [recent studies suggest](https://www.thelancet.com/journals/lancet/article/PIIS0140-6736(22)00090-3/fulltext) that even booster shots are "insufficient \[for] prevention of symptomatic infection in otherwise healthy individuals".
